# Engineering Assumptions

CemGuard is built to demonstrate modern industrial reliability engineering, IoT telemetry processing, and executive decision-support systems.

To facilitate evaluation without requiring access to proprietary industrial data (e.g., Dangote Cement's actual SCADA databases), the platform relies on the following engineering assumptions:

1. **Simulated Telemetry**: All sensor values (Temperature, Vibration, Power, Current) are dynamically generated by the `Telemetry Engine`. They are derived from authentic baselines but are strictly simulated for demonstration purposes.
2. **Equipment Thresholds**: The operational limits defined in the `Equipment Profiles` for Crushers, Kilns, Mills, and Packers are representative of heavy industry standards but are not exact proprietary specifications.
3. **Weibull Distribution**: The reliability calculations (Probability of Failure) utilize simplified Weibull distribution parameters. The shape ($\beta$) and scale ($\eta$) parameters are illustrative.
4. **Maintenance Effectiveness**: The system assumes theoretical restoration values for maintenance strategies (e.g., Condition-Based Maintenance restores 100% health, Corrective Maintenance restores 62%). Real-world restoration varies by exact execution.
5. **Cost and Impact Figures**: Production value (e.g., ₦25,000/hr), repair costs, and CO₂ emissions are hypothetical baseline figures used to demonstrate the translation of mechanical failure into executive business impact.
6. **Decision Support Focus**: The primary purpose of the simulator is to demonstrate the software architecture of a Decision Support System (DSS) utilizing Reliability Centered Maintenance (RCM) logic, rather than acting as a perfectly calibrated Digital Twin.
